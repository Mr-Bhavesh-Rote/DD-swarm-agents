01 · About

What is Deep-DD?

Deep-DD is a US-compliance adverse due-diligence research platform. It is not a financial analysis tool or a background-check product. Its singular mandate is to hunt for derogatory issues — sanctions, litigation, corruption, weapons supply chains, human-rights abuses, environmental harm, and adverse media — and surface them with source-verified citations.

Not investment research. Not a credit check.
Deep-DD is a compliance & adverse screening tool. Every report carries an explicit disclaimer: "Decision-support instrument — not a background-check product. Requires human review before action."

The platform operates on two subject types: company and individual. For companies, it maps corporate structure, beneficial owners, sanctions exposure, litigation history, and supply-chain controversies. For individuals, it covers identity, career, legal/regulatory history, PEP status, controversies, and net worth flags.

Two planning modes give flexibility: Template Mode uses fixed YAML agent definitions for fast, reproducible, bounded-cost runs; AI-Tailored Mode generates a custom swarm per task description for complex investigations where a standard template falls short.

02 · Business & Objective

Why This Platform Exists

Compliance due-diligence is a high-stakes, labor-intensive process. A single adverse due-diligence report on a counterparty can take a team of analysts 2–5 days. Deep-DD compresses that to under an hour, without sacrificing citation discipline or source quality.

Problem

Manual Research Is Slow & Inconsistent

Analysts spend hours searching OFAC lists, PACER records, news archives, and ESG databases — each search done differently, with variable thoroughness and no structured citation trail.

Risk

Missed Red Flags Have Real Consequences

Onboarding a sanctioned entity, an FCPA violator, or a weapons supplier without proper due-diligence exposes firms to regulatory fines, reputational damage, and criminal liability.

Solution

Verified AI Research with Citation Discipline

Deep-DD mandates source citation for every claim, enforces a faithfulness threshold, and queries compliance databases that human analysts may miss or skip under time pressure.

Core business objective: Reduce adverse due-diligence research time from days to under an hour, while increasing coverage of compliance databases and enforcing citation discipline that manual processes rarely achieve. The platform is decision-support — a human analyst always reviews before export.

03 · Why & How It Is Needed

The Compliance Gap Deep-DD Closes

Global enforcement of sanctions, anti-corruption laws, and ESG standards has accelerated sharply since 2022. The burden on compliance teams has grown faster than headcount. Deep-DD addresses five structural gaps that existing solutions leave open.

Gap 1 · Coverage

Most Tools Miss Non-SDN Databases

Many compliance checks query only the OFAC SDN List. Deep-DD mandates searches across OFAC Non-SDN, BIS Entity List, UN Sanctions, EU Consolidated Sanctions, PACER federal courts, EPA ECHO, OSHA enforcement, FPDS government contracts, OCCRP organized crime index, and Who Profits — all in one run.

Gap 2 · Citation Discipline

Ungrounded AI Summaries Are Dangerous

Generic LLM summaries hallucinate details or omit sources. Deep-DD requires [n] citation markers for every claim, runs a verifier LLM to check each citation, and enforces a 0.85 faithfulness threshold before publishing.

Gap 3 · Speed

Manual DD Takes Days — Markets Move in Hours

Supply-chain disruptions, sanctions designations, and media events surface faster than manual processes can respond. Deep-DD's parallel agent swarm compresses multi-day research to under an hour.

Gap 4 · Chain-Search

Connected Entities Are the Real Risk

The subject itself is rarely sanctioned — it's a subsidiary, a board member's other company, or a shipping partner. Deep-DD's CHAIN-SEARCH protocol requires agents to search for every discovered connected entity, not just the primary subject.

Gap 5 · Audit Trail

Regulators Demand Evidence, Not Summaries

When a regulator asks how a risk was assessed, "the AI said so" is not acceptable. Deep-DD produces RAW reports (per-agent tool call logs, source URLs, tool execution traces) alongside FINAL verified reports, creating a defensible audit trail.

How it works in one sentence: Five specialized AI agents run in parallel, each querying its domain's compliance databases and the open web, while a verifier LLM cross-checks every citation against the actual source text before the report is released.

Key Architecture Decisions

1
Reducer-Merged State Channels for Safe Parallelism
LangGraph uses operator.add reducers on list channels (raw_outputs, findings, sources_raw). Parallel branches safely append without write conflicts — no locking, no merge logic needed at the application layer.
GraphState: raw_outputs: Annotated[list, operator.add] # safe parallel append
2
Two-Phase Dispatch for Entity Context Injection
Phase 1 (overview agent) must complete before Phase 2 begins. The Entity Extractor node pulls discovered entities (subsidiaries, officers, shareholders) from Phase 1 and injects them into Phase 2 agent prompts — enabling CHAIN-SEARCH on connected entities, not just the primary subject.
3
Postgres Checkpointer for Resumability
Every LangGraph super-step is snapshotted to PostgreSQL. If a worker crashes mid-run, the run can resume from the last completed node. Research already done is not repeated. This is critical for long-running runs where Tavily deep-search can add significant per-agent time.
4
Forced Search Fallback Gate
After each research agent completes, the system checks acceptance gates: ≥3 tool calls, ≥1 web_search, findings > 0. If any gate fails, the system auto-executes domain-specific compliance queries and re-processes the LLM output. This prevents "empty agent" failures from degrading report quality silently.
5
Global Citation Registry with Deduplication
The Aggregator builds a single global citation registry keyed by canonical URL. If two agents discover the same source, they share one citation number [n] in the FINAL report. This prevents the same article appearing as [3], [7], and [12] across sections.
citations.py: dedup_by_canonical_url() → assign stable [1]…[n] ids
6
Revise Loop with Max Revision Guard
If the Verifier's faithfulness score falls below 0.85, it routes unsupported claims back to the Synthesizer with specific feedback. The loop runs up to MAX_REVISIONS times (default 2). After that, the report is flagged needs_review rather than auto-published — preventing infinite loops while preserving human oversight.
7
Server-Driven Model Resolution
Model selection follows a four-level precedence chain: per-agent override → per-role default → run-level global default → system default (Sonnet 4.6). This lets power users dial up to Opus on specific agents (e.g., Synthesizer) while keeping cheaper models on high-volume nodes (e.g., research agents).
per-agent → per-role → global default → system default (sonnet-4-6)
8
RQ Worker Pool for Concurrent Reports
Each report run is enqueued as an RQ job. A configurable worker pool (WORKER_CONCURRENCY, default 3) processes jobs in parallel. Workers are stateless — they pull state from PostgreSQL/Redis, run the LangGraph pipeline, and push results back. Scaling is horizontal: add more worker containers.

05b · Planning Modes

Standard vs. AI-Tailored Mode

Every run starts with a planning step that determines how many agents run, what each agent hunts, and which tools it uses. Deep-DD offers two fundamentally different planning approaches — choose based on your task complexity and cost tolerance.

STANDARD
Template Mode

Loads a fixed YAML agent definition from config/agents.company.yaml or config/agents.individual.yaml. The swarm is always exactly 5 agents, each with a pre-defined role, tool set, and goal. Fast, reproducible, bounded cost — every run on the same subject type runs the same swarm.

✓ Deterministic — same agent swarm every time
✓ Fast startup — no planner LLM call needed
✓ Bounded cost — predictable per-run spend
✓ Audit-friendly — swarm config is versioned in YAML
✗ Fixed coverage — cannot add niche agents for unusual subjects
✗ No task-specific optimization per investigation

AI-TAILORED
Custom Swarm Mode

The Planner LLM reads the task description and generates a custom WorkflowPlan — choosing how many agents to spawn (1 to MAX_SUBAGENTS), what each one is named, what goal it pursues, and which tools it gets. There is no fixed agent count. The number is bounded only by MAX_SUBAGENTS (configurable, default 5, no hard ceiling) and your run budget. Need 12 specialized agents for a complex multi-entity investigation? Set MAX_SUBAGENTS=12 and let the Planner decide.

✓ Unlimited agents — 1 to N, bounded only by MAX_SUBAGENTS config
✓ Task-optimized — Planner invents agent names, goals, and tool lists
✓ Domain guarantee — _ensure_domain_coverage() auto-injects missing compliance domains
✓ Flexible for complex investigations: arms networks, offshore structures, multi-entity chains
✗ Higher cost — Planner LLM call + more agent runs = larger spend
✗ Non-deterministic — swarm structure varies per run (requires plan approval step)

07 · Quality & Verification System

How Deep-DD Ensures Report Accuracy

Citation discipline is the platform's core differentiator. The multi-tier verification system ensures that every claim in a FINAL report is traceable to a real source, and that the source actually supports the claim.

≥ 85%
Faithfulness Threshold
Of all cited claims, at least 85% must be confirmed as directly supported by the cited source text. Below this threshold, the report routes back to the Synthesizer for revision.
Coverage
Citation Coverage Score
Every claim must either carry a [n] citation or be labelled UNVERIFIED. Coverage = (cited + labelled) / total claims. Claims with no citation and no label are flagged as gaps.
Layer 1 · Per-Claim

LLM-as-Judge Faithfulness Check

The Verifier processes 8 claims per LLM call, comparing each claim to the actual source text stored in the database. It classifies each claim as: supported / partially_supported / unsupported / cannot_verify.

Layer 2 · Source Quality

Source Tier Classification

Every source is classified: Primary (government databases, court records), Secondary (established news outlets, NGO reports), or Tertiary (blogs, forums, social media). High-severity claims must be backed by Tier 1 or Tier 2 sources.

Layer 3 · Logic Checks

Circular Dependency Detection

The quality gate flags self-referencing claims — e.g., "Company X is corrupt" where the only source is Company X's own press release. Wikipedia is explicitly excluded as a source; agents must find and cite the underlying primary source.

What happens when verification fails? If faithfulness < 0.85 after MAX_REVISIONS (default: 2 revise loops), the report is marked needs_review and flagged for mandatory human analyst review before any export is permitted. No low-confidence report auto-publishes.

09 · Deployment & Operations

Running Deep-DD

The platform supports three deployment modes: local development, Docker Compose for team instances, and headless CLI for scripted or automated runs.

Mode 1

Local Development

Use scripts/setup/setup-all.sh to install dependencies, setup-fresh.sh to configure .env and run DB migrations, then run-local.sh to start API + workers + Vite frontend.

Mode 2

Docker Compose

Run docker compose up --build for all services. Scale workers horizontally with docker compose up --scale worker=N where N is the desired concurrent report capacity.

Mode 3

Headless CLI

Run python cli.py --subject "Company Name" --type company for scripted runs with no API, DB, or UI. Outputs report JSON to stdout. Useful for batch processing or CI integration.

10 · Glossary

Key Terms

Terminology used throughout the platform and this primer, organized for quick reference.

SDN
Specially Designated National — entity on OFAC's primary sanctions list. US persons are prohibited from all transactions with SDNs.
OFAC
Office of Foreign Assets Control — US Treasury agency that administers sanctions programs and the SDN list.
BIS
Bureau of Industry and Security — US Commerce agency that controls exports of dual-use goods. Maintains Entity List and Denied Parties lists.
PACER
Public Access to Court Electronic Records — US federal court filing system. Source for civil, criminal, and bankruptcy records.
FCPA
Foreign Corrupt Practices Act — US law prohibiting bribery of foreign officials. Applies to US persons and companies listed on US exchanges.
PEP
Politically Exposed Person — individual holding or having held public office. Requires enhanced due-diligence under AML regulations.
UBO
Ultimate Beneficial Owner — natural person(s) who ultimately own or control an entity (typically ≥25% ownership threshold).
OCCRP
Organized Crime and Corruption Reporting Project — investigative journalism network. ALEPH database indexes leaks, court records, and corporate registries.
LangGraph
Python framework for building stateful, multi-actor LLM applications using directed acyclic graphs (DAGs). Used for Deep-DD's orchestration pipeline.
Faithfulness Score
Ratio of LLM-cited claims confirmed as supported by their source text. Deep-DD requires ≥0.85 before publishing a FINAL report.
RAW Report
Per-agent unedited narrative, all discovered sources, and full tool call logs. Used for audit trail and human review, not client delivery.
FINAL Report
Verified, consolidated report with [n] citation markers, faithfulness scores, source manifest, and structured sections. The deliverable report.
SSE
Server-Sent Events — HTTP streaming protocol used by Deep-DD to push real-time per-agent status updates to the frontend without polling.
CHAIN-SEARCH
Deep-DD protocol requiring agents to search for every connected entity (subsidiaries, officers, shareholders) discovered during primary research, not just the named subject.
EPA ECHO
Environmental Compliance History Online — EPA database of enforcement actions, fines, penalties, and consent decrees for environmental violations.
FPDS
Federal Procurement Data System — US government database of all federal contracts. Used to identify defense, weapons, or dual-use procurement relationships.
